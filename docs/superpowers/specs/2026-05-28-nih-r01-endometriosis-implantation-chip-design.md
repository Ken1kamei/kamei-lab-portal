# NIH R01 Grant Package Design: Endometriosis-Associated Implantation Failure on Chip

## Purpose

This specification defines the document package and scientific architecture for a full 5-year NIH parent R01 application targeted for the October 5, 2026 new R01 receipt date.

The application will center on the project concept: "Endometriosis-associated implantation failure modeled with a human blastoid-endometrium-on-chip platform."

The proposal should be written as a mechanism-first reproductive biology R01. The chip platform, hPSC-derived blastoids, imaging, secretome, transcriptomic or marker readouts, and quantitative scorecard all support the central biological question: how endometriosis-associated inflammatory and receptivity-disrupting states impair human blastoid attachment and retention.

## Funding Opportunity and Working Constraints

- Working opportunity: NIH Research Project Grant, Parent R01, Clinical Trial Not Allowed, PA-25-301.
- Working deadline: October 5, 2026, for a new R01 application.
- Working institute fit: primary alignment with NICHD, especially fertility, infertility, uterine function, implantation biology, and gynecologic health/endometriosis interests.
- Secondary programmatic resonance: NIBIB or NCATS may be relevant because the project uses microphysiological systems, but the application should not read as tool development alone.
- Maximum project period: 5 years.
- Research Strategy limit: 12 pages unless the active NOFO or related notice changes.
- Specific Aims limit: 1 page.
- Project Summary/Abstract: 30 lines of text.
- Project Narrative: 3 sentences.
- Data Management and Sharing Plan: required because the project will generate scientific data.
- Clinical trial status: not allowed; the project must avoid proposing prospective assignment of human participants to interventions for health-related outcomes.

## Recommended Scientific Frame

The proposal should argue that endometriosis-associated infertility remains difficult to mechanistically dissect because implantation is a dynamic human tissue-embryo interaction, existing models incompletely capture the uterine microenvironment, and clinical samples are confounded by disease stage, menstrual phase, treatment exposure, and embryo variability.

The application will solve this gap by building a controlled human blastoid-endometrium-on-chip platform that can separate three interacting variables:

- Endometrial receptive state.
- Endometriosis-relevant inflammatory or lesion-derived perturbation state.
- Blastoid attachment and retention behavior under controlled microfluidic conditions.

The chip should be presented as the experimental engine for causal tests, not as the final endpoint. The platform's value is that it makes implantation failure measurable, perturbable, and interpretable using dynamic imaging, effluent secretome sampling, and molecular validation.

## Grant Angle Selected

### Recommended Angle: Mechanism-First Reproductive Biology R01

The application should lead with the disease and mechanism:

"Endometriosis-associated inflammatory remodeling of the endometrial environment disrupts receptive epithelial-stromal states needed for blastoid attachment and retention."

The platform then becomes the way to test this hypothesis under human-relevant, controlled, repeatable conditions.

### De-emphasized Alternative: Technology-First MPS R01

The technology-first framing is not the main strategy because reviewers may perceive the application as platform development without sufficient disease biology. Engineering advances should appear in Innovation and Approach, but the overall impact should come from endometriosis, infertility, and implantation biology.

### De-emphasized Alternative: Rescue-First Translational R01

Rescue experiments should appear as part of Aim 3, but the application should not overpromise therapeutic discovery before the assay and disease-state model are validated.

## Central Hypothesis

Endometriosis-associated inflammatory and receptivity-disrupting programs impair human blastoid attachment and retention by altering epithelial-stromal signaling, adhesion-permissive surface states, and cytokine/prostaglandin/ECM remodeling dynamics; these mechanisms can be isolated and quantified in a controlled human blastoid-endometrium-on-chip platform.

## Specific Aims Architecture

### Aim 1: Build and validate a hormone-conditioned human endometrium-on-chip that supports reproducible blastoid attachment and retention under flow.

Purpose: establish the baseline receptive chip assay.

Expected elements:

- Generate or prepare endometrial epithelial and stromal components, preferably with hPSC-derived or organoid-compatible sources where feasible.
- Establish microfluidic epithelial-stromal architecture, hormone conditioning, flow compatibility, imaging access, and effluent collection.
- Define receptive-state QC using morphology, viability, epithelial continuity, stromal support, selected receptivity markers, and secretome features.
- Introduce quality-scored hPSC-derived blastoids and quantify attachment frequency, time-to-attachment, retention duration, positional stability, and response to defined flow challenge.

Success criterion: reproducible blastoid attachment and retention in receptive chips without tissue collapse, nonspecific trapping, or unacceptable blastoid quality confounding.

### Aim 2: Define how endometriosis-relevant inflammatory programs disrupt endometrial receptivity and blastoid attachment dynamics.

Purpose: create interpretable disease-context chips and test whether endometriosis-associated perturbations shift implantation behavior.

Expected elements:

- Prioritize inflammatory and lesion-relevant cues such as IL-1beta, TNF-alpha, IL-6, PGE2, macrophage-conditioned media, lesion-conditioned media, or patient-derived sample-informed cue sets, depending on available materials.
- Tune dose, exposure timing, and sequencing relative to hormone conditioning.
- Measure viability, morphology, barrier or epithelial integrity, stromal state, secretome shifts, and receptivity markers before introducing blastoids.
- Run paired healthy receptive versus disease-context chip experiments using matched blastoid batches.
- Quantify shifts in attachment frequency, attachment timing, retention, contact stability, and post-challenge secretome trajectories.

Success criterion: identify at least one reproducible endometriosis-relevant perturbation state that preserves assay interpretability while impairing, delaying, destabilizing, or otherwise altering blastoid attachment and retention.

### Aim 3: Integrate imaging, secretome, and molecular readouts into a quantitative implantation-failure scorecard and test rescue strategies.

Purpose: convert the assay into an interpretable model that can rank disease states and evaluate candidate rescue conditions.

Expected elements:

- Build a transparent scorecard from chip condition, perturbation state, hormone state, flow parameters, blastoid quality, live imaging features, secretome features, and selected molecular markers.
- Use the scorecard to define an attachment/retention index and inflammatory-receptivity state index.
- Test targeted rescue conditions such as anti-inflammatory modulation, LIF/receptivity support, hormone-timing correction, prostaglandin-pathway modulation, or ECM/adhesion pathway modulation.
- Validate whether rescue improves receptive-state markers and blastoid attachment/retention behavior without simply masking tissue injury.

Success criterion: produce an interpretable quantitative framework that ranks healthy, disease-context, and rescued conditions and identifies candidate drivers of implantation failure.

## Research Strategy Design

### Significance

The Significance section should establish:

- Endometriosis is a major contributor to reproductive morbidity and infertility.
- Endometriosis-associated implantation failure remains mechanistically unresolved.
- Current models do not capture dynamic human blastoid-endometrial interaction under controlled flow and inflammatory context.
- A human blastoid-endometrium-on-chip model can directly test disease-relevant endometrial states while controlling embryo-like structure quality, hormone conditioning, timing, and flow.
- The work will produce both mechanistic insight and a reusable experimental platform for future patient-specific and therapeutic studies.

### Innovation

The Innovation section should emphasize:

- A dynamic human blastoid-endometrium-on-chip assay for implantation failure.
- Separation of receptive-state biology, endometriosis-like inflammatory perturbation, and blastoid attachment behavior.
- Integration of live imaging, effluent secretome, and molecular QC into a single quantitative scorecard.
- Use of hPSC-derived blastoids to avoid reliance on human embryos while modeling key implantation-adjacent behaviors.
- A staged digital-twin concept that begins as a transparent scorecard rather than an overfit black-box model.

### Approach

The Approach should be written around the three aims. Each aim should include:

- Rationale.
- Experimental design.
- Key variables and controls.
- Quantitative endpoints.
- Statistical and analysis strategy.
- Expected outcomes.
- Alternative strategies.
- Milestones and go/no-go criteria.

The Approach should repeatedly reassure reviewers that the project is ambitious but controlled: first validate the baseline receptive chip, then tune interpretable disease-context perturbations, then integrate and rescue.

## Required Controls and Rigor Features

The package must include the following control logic:

- Healthy receptive chip with blastoid challenge.
- Receptive chip without blastoid to measure baseline secretome drift.
- Disease-context chip without blastoid to separate perturbation effects from blastoid-driven changes.
- Non-receptive hormone condition as a negative implantation control.
- Flow-only and inert-particle controls to distinguish biological attachment from trapping.
- Blastoid quality scoring before chip introduction.
- Matched blastoid batches across paired chip conditions.
- Viability, morphology, epithelial/stromal integrity, and flow compatibility gates before blastoid challenge.
- Biological and technical replicate structure.
- Prespecified primary and secondary endpoints.
- Sex as a biological variable rationale, noting the reproductive tissue context and the source of cells/materials.
- Authentication plan for key biological and chemical resources.

## Five-Year Project Logic

- Year 1: Baseline receptive chip validation and blastoid attachment assay.
- Year 2: Receptive-state QC, reproducibility, and expanded controls.
- Year 3: Endometriosis-relevant perturbation tuning and paired disease-context comparisons.
- Year 4: Integrated imaging, secretome, and molecular scorecard development.
- Year 5: Rescue-condition testing, platform validation, dataset release, and preparation for patient-specific expansion.

## Major Risks and Mitigations

### Risk: Baseline attachment assay is inconsistent.

Mitigation: tighten blastoid quality scoring, hormone conditioning, flow settings, chip geometry, and receptive-state QC; use non-receptive and inert-particle controls to separate biological failure from technical artifacts.

### Risk: Inflammatory perturbation causes nonspecific tissue injury.

Mitigation: tune perturbations against viability, morphology, epithelial integrity, secretome induction, and flow compatibility before blastoid challenge.

### Risk: Disease-context effects are small or variable.

Mitigation: use paired designs, matched blastoid batches, repeated perturbation states, sensitive dynamic endpoints, and molecular confirmation of receptive-state shifts.

### Risk: Scorecard becomes overfit or too computationally ambitious.

Mitigation: begin with transparent, prespecified indices; only advance to predictive modeling after repeated experiments support stable feature-outcome relationships.

### Risk: Reviewer concern about blastoid ethics or model validity.

Mitigation: clearly describe blastoid governance, institutional approvals, developmental limits, non-embryo status where applicable, and the biological scope of inference.

## Success Definition

The completed grant package should give reviewers a clear, fundable story:

Endometriosis-associated infertility is an important unresolved problem; implantation failure is difficult to study in humans; this project creates a controlled human blastoid-endometrium-on-chip platform to test how disease-relevant inflammatory and receptivity-disrupting programs impair attachment and retention; and the work will produce rigorous mechanisms, a reusable scorecard, and a foundation for future patient-specific and therapeutic studies.

The document package is complete when the user has usable drafts for all core scientific sections, compliance/resource sections, review-support materials, and collaborator templates needed to assemble an NIH R01 application through the institutional submission route.
